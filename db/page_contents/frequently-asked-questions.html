1. What is the Learning Revolution?
The Learning Revolution aims to open up more opportunities for people of all ages to learn for pleasure and raise awareness of the mental and physical benefits that continuing to learn brings people.

It embraces the whole spectrum of informal adult learning – learning from websites like Wikipedia, art classes, book clubs, car maintenance, rambling, singing and dancing.

2. What is informal adult learning?
Informal adult learning is non-accredited learning for pleasure, development and fulfilment. It can be a stepping stone to further learning, qualifications and more rewarding work. It can help keep people mentally and physically active and independent into old age. It can bring people and communities together.

3. Why get involved with the Learning Revolution?
By coming together with other organisations under the banner of The Learning Revolution, you’ll help lifelong learning gain a higher profile and greater influence with the public and private sectors going forward – helping organisations and people like you to find more resources and make contact with more learners.

4. Who is The Learning Revolution aimed at?
We want to involve individuals and their communities who are currently or could be learning for pleasure, across all ages and from all backgrounds. We particularly want to encourage those who may not see themselves as potential learners, for whatever reason. Our aim is to inspire people to take part in all types of activities related to learning for pleasure, both structured and self-organised. By continuing to learn, they will build new skills and knowledge, discover new hobbies and interests, and share their passions with others like them.

5. What is the Learning Revolution Festival?
We all like to learn new things, go to new places and meet new people. The Learning Revolution Festival celebrates all kinds of informal adult learning and the difference learning makes to individuals, families, communities and organisations.

Throughout October, hundreds of events will highlight the wide variety of learning that’s taking place. The month will give people who are already taking part in informal learning a chance to celebrate what they enjoy and will give others the opportunity to try something new.

Libraries, galleries, adult education providers, sports clubs, voluntary organisations, self-organised groups and many other organisations can get involved by showcasing activities that are already available and offering, or signposting, new activities to get people started.

6. How long is the Festival?
The Festival takes place throughout October with hundreds of open events all over the country – you can look at the ‘news section’ of the website or use the search engine calendar to find out more.

7. Where will it take place?
Events will take place anywhere and everywhere across England – we are encouraging everyone from carpentry classes to aerobics groups and knitting circles to throw open their doors and let people try their hand at new and exciting activities.

8. How can I take part in the Festival?
You can attend as many events as you can fit into your diary all throughout October. If you are an organiser you can submit your October event to be included or visit the Ning site where you will find lots of publicity materials to help you promote your event. You can submit your event online at the website by clicking the ‘Promote your event’ tab.

9. Do I have to pay to attend an event?
Many though not all of the events will be free. You can use the search engine to find cheap and free events near to you.

10. Will there be something going on near me?
Using the search engine you can put in your postcode and see what’s going on near you.

You can also call our helpline on 0800 100 900 if you can find what you’re looking for or would like to know how learning for pleasure can help you in other areas of your life.

11. Can I find an activity for me my children to attend?
The Learning Revolution Festival includes classes and activities for people of all ages, some of which are aimed specifically at families.

12. What will happen after the Festival?
In the longer term the website will become a place to search for longer learning activities for example a 6 week drawing class, karate or riding lessons.

14. What is the Learning Revolution Pledge?
To support The Learning Revolution, we want to encourage a range of public, private and voluntary sector organisations to actively promote learning and its benefits. We are asking organisations of all sizes to sign up to The Learning Revolution Pledge to show their commitment to bringing The Learning Revolution vision to life. These organisations will act as ambassadors and advocates for lifelong learning, driving change on the ground and in local communities.
